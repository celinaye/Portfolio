Overview

I observed the Courses at Brown (C@B) interface, which allows Brown University students to register for and browse courses with ease. I explored the key components of the interface and made observations based on user interviews to then create personas and a storyboard.



Sketch

I sketched out the interface to understand the key components of C@B, which include the sign in button that allows students to sign in to their Brown University account, a search bar to find specific courses, and a filtering systen that includes a drop down and check boxes, all of which are noted in blue in the sketch.

Observations

I observed 3 people using the interface, taking notes and paying careful attention to how they use C@B to make objective observations. Afterwards, I had each person answer a series of questions and summarized the responses below.

Key Observations
  • All users used the search bar feature and the ability to add to the primary cart.
  • One user did not use any filter options for classes, while the other two users used the WRIT (writing requirement) and FYS (first-year seminar) filters respectively.
  • Two users added courses to saved carts while the other user only utilized the primary cart.
  • Two users added and removed courses from their carts quicker than the other user.
Questions Asked
  • Have you ever used C@B before?
  • Tell us about your experience using the C@B interface.
  • On a scale of 1-5 with 1 being easy/smooth and 5 being difficult, how would you rate the C@B interface?
  • Overall, how did you react to using the C@B interface?
  • What features did you interact with the most?
  • What features did you interact with the least?
Summarized Responses
  • All users have used C@B at least once before and thought it was easy to add and remove classes, however, less familiar users were not aware of specific filters for classes.
  • Two users rated the C@B interface a 2 out of 5 while the third user rated it a 1 out of 5 in terms of how easy or difficult the C@B interface was to use.
  • All users thought the C@B interface did what it was built to do and is for the most part, fairly intuitive.
  • Two users interacted with the filtering features specifically and the ability to add classes to the primary cart the most while the third user used the search bar feature the most.
  • All users used the "Suggested Courses" features the least.

Personas

After collecting data from the observations and interviews, I created two personas based on overarching trends observed amongst the interviewees. Each of the personas consist of a four-quadrant empathy map, describing what users think, feel, say, and do.



Storyboard

Lastly, I created a storyboard for one of the personas, Annoyed Abby, to capture the persona's journey from start to end while reflecting the goals of the persona and all necessary steps for using C@B.

Conclusion

From the personas and storyboarding project, I took away the importance of understanding different user perspectives. Following the user's entire journey helped identify what worked well and what could be improved to increase the usability of an interface.
